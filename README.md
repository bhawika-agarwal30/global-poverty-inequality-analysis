# Mapping Inequality: Global Poverty and Income Distribution Analysis

## Overview
This project analyses global poverty and income inequality trends across regions and income groups over time. It focuses on poverty gaps, regional disparities, socioeconomic mobility, and long-term development patterns.

## Objectives
- Examine poverty gap trends across major world regions
- Study changes in income inequality across income groups
- Understand the impact of economic reforms, globalization, and welfare policies
- Analyse long-term patterns in mobility and development

## Methodology
- Used comparative and trend-based analysis on global datasets
- Studied regional poverty gap changes from 1980 to 2019
- Examined income inequality across low, middle, and high-income groups
- Interpreted mobility matrices and growth incidence patterns

## Key Insights
- East Asia and South Asia showed significant decline in poverty gaps over time
- Sub-Saharan Africa recorded the highest and most volatile poverty gap
- High-income countries maintained consistently low poverty and inequality levels
- Economic reforms, industrialization, and social protection policies played a major role in reducing inequality

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Data Analysis
- Comparative Trend Analysis
- Economic Interpretation
